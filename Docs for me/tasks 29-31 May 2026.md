## Reference — original strategy

If the product is ready, the code is stable, and the blocker is purely marketing, you are in a classic founder trap: **You are trying to solve a distribution problem with engineering mindsets.**

Do not spend another minute optimizing the platform. We need to build a high-leverage marketing engine that feeds users into Preppeo. Since you can vibe-code anything instantly, your biggest advantage is **Engineering as Marketing**—building micro-tools that drive organic traffic.

### Play 1: The "Question of the Day" Embed Engine
A single, beautifully designed widget on Preppeo hosting a daily "Hardest SAT Question." Public landing page, no signup to solve. Auto-cycle a fresh question at midnight GMT. Below it, a blurred "AI Concept Breakdown" — to unblur the deep report / ask the AI tutor *why* option B is wrong, the student enters their email. Warm, high-intent leads daily.

### Play 2: Programmatic Reddit & Discord Scraping
SAT students live on r/SAT and student Discords, posting screenshots asking for shortcuts. Don't spam — get alerted when keywords like "hard math question", "Bluebook mock 4", "Desmos shortcut" trend, then jump in as the expert founder with a clean explanation + a soft pointer to Preppeo's free tool.

### Play 3: The "Desmos Calculator" Trap (Programmatic SEO)
Students want Desmos hacks to solve in 5 seconds. Build 50–100 targeted landing pages for terms like "How to use Desmos for SAT circles". Layout: embedded GIF of the hack → a practice question → CTA to Preppeo's adaptive modules ($99/mo).

> **Reality check:** ~10,000 free test-takers ≈ 300 paid subscribers.
