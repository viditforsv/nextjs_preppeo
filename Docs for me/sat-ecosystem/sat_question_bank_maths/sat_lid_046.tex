\question
In the figure below, $\triangle ABC$ is similar to $\triangle DEF$. If the ratio of the length of side $AB$ to the length of side $DE$ is $2:5$, and the length of side $BC$ is 8, what is the length of side $EF$?
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.6]
    \draw[thick] (0,0) node[anchor=north]{$A$} -- (3,0) node[anchor=north]{$C$} -- (1,2) node[anchor=south]{$B$} -- cycle;
    \begin{scope}[shift={(5,0)}]
    \draw[thick] (0,0) node[anchor=north]{$D$} -- (7.5,0) node[anchor=north]{$F$} -- (2.5,5) node[anchor=south]{$E$} -- cycle;
    \end{scope}
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
\begin{tasks}(2)
    \task 3.2
    \task 10
    \task 16
    \task 20
\end{tasks}

\begin{solution}
\textbf{Conceptual Explanation:} \\
When two triangles are similar, the ratios of their corresponding sides are equal. We can set up a proportion using the given scale factor of $2:5$.

\vspace{10pt}
\textbf{Calculation and Logic:} \\
The ratio is $\frac{AB}{DE} = \frac{BC}{EF}$. \\
Substituting the known values: $\frac{2}{5} = \frac{8}{EF}$. \\
Cross-multiplying gives $2(EF) = 40$. \\
Dividing by 2, we find $EF = 20$.

\vspace{15pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{D}
\end{solution}

\question
In the figure below, $\overline{BD}$ is parallel to $\overline{AE}$. If $CD = 6$, $DE = 3$, and $BC = 8$, what is the length of segment $AB$?
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.8]
    \draw[thick] (0,0) node[anchor=north]{$C$} -- (6,0) node[anchor=north]{$E$};
    \draw[thick] (0,0) -- (6,3) node[anchor=south]{$A$};
    \draw[thick] (4,0) node[anchor=north]{$D$} -- (4,2) node[anchor=south west]{$B$};
    \draw[thick] (6,0) -- (6,3);
    \node at (2,-0.4) {6};
    \node at (5,-0.4) {3};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
\begin{tasks}(2)
    \task 4
    \task 12
    \task 15
    \task 24
\end{tasks}

\begin{solution}
\textbf{Conceptual Explanation:} \\
Because $\overline{BD} \parallel \overline{AE}$, $\triangle CBD$ is similar to $\triangle CAE$ by Angle-Angle similarity. Note that the side $CE$ is the sum of $CD$ and $DE$.

\vspace{10pt}
\textbf{Calculation and Logic:} \\
First, find $CE$: $6 + 3 = 9$. \\
The ratio of the sides is $\frac{CD}{CE} = \frac{BC}{AC}$. \\
$\frac{6}{9} = \frac{8}{AC} \implies \frac{2}{3} = \frac{8}{AC}$. \\
$2(AC) = 24 \implies AC = 12$. \\
The question asks for $AB$, which is $AC - BC$. \\
$AB = 12 - 8 = 4$.

\vspace{15pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{A}
\end{solution}

\question
In right triangle $ABC$, an altitude is drawn from the right angle $C$ to the hypotenuse $AB$, intersecting at point $D$. If $AD = 4$ and $DB = 9$, what is the length of the altitude $CD$?

\begin{solution}
\textbf{Conceptual Explanation:} \\
When an altitude is drawn to the hypotenuse of a right triangle, it creates two smaller triangles that are both similar to the original triangle and to each other. This allows us to use the Geometric Mean theorem: $CD^2 = AD \times DB$.

\vspace{10pt}
\textbf{Calculation and Logic:} \\
Using the similarity ratio $\frac{AD}{CD} = \frac{CD}{DB}$: \\
$CD^2 = 4 \times 9$. \\
$CD^2 = 36$. \\
$CD = 6$.

\vspace{15pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{6}
\end{solution}

\question
A 6-foot tall man stands so that his shadow exactly overlaps the end of the shadow cast by a 24-foot tall flagpole. If the man is 30 feet away from the flagpole, how long is the man's shadow, in feet?
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.15]
    \draw[thick] (0,0) -- (40,0);
    \draw[ultra thick] (0,24) -- (0,0) node[midway, left]{24};
    \draw[thick] (30,6) -- (30,0) node[midway, right]{6};
    \draw[dashed] (0,24) -- (40,0);
    \node at (20, -3) {30};
    \node at (35, -3) {$s$};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
\begin{tasks}(2)
    \task 6
    \task 10
    \task 12
    \task 15
\end{tasks}

\begin{solution}
\textbf{Conceptual Explanation:} \\
The flagpole and the man create two nested similar right triangles because they both stand perpendicular to the ground and share the angle of the sun's rays. Let $s$ be the length of the man's shadow. The total base of the large triangle is $30 + s$.

\vspace{10pt}
\textbf{Calculation and Logic:} \\
Set up the proportion: $\frac{\text{Height of Man}}{\text{Height of Flagpole}} = \frac{\text{Shadow of Man}}{\text{Total Base}}$ \\
$\frac{6}{24} = \frac{s}{30 + s}$ \\
$\frac{1}{4} = \frac{s}{30 + s}$ \\
$30 + s = 4s \implies 30 = 3s \implies s = 10$.

\vspace{15pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{B}
\end{solution}

\question
The ratio of the lengths of corresponding sides of two similar triangles is $3:7$. If the area of the smaller triangle is 18, what is the area of the larger triangle?

\begin{solution}
\textbf{Conceptual Explanation:} \\
For similar figures, if the side ratio is $a:b$, the area ratio is $a^2:b^2$. This is a consistent rule for all similar shapes on the SAT.

\vspace{10pt}
\textbf{Calculation and Logic:} \\
Side ratio $= 3:7$. \\
Area ratio $= 3^2:7^2 = 9:49$. \\
Set up the proportion: $\frac{9}{49} = \frac{18}{\text{Area}_{large}}$. \\
Since $9 \times 2 = 18$, we find $\text{Area}_{large} = 49 \times 2 = 98$.

\vspace{15pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{98}
\end{solution}
