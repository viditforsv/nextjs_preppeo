% Q1: Based on Park Area / Squared Units (Very Hard)
\question
A national forest has a total area of $15,488,000$ square yards. A researcher needs to report this area in square miles for a federal document. Given that $1$ mile is equal to $1,760$ yards, which of the following is the best estimate of the forest's area in square miles?
\begin{tasks}(2)
    \task 5.00
    \task 8.80
    \task 25.00
    \task 30.98
\end{tasks}

\begin{solution}
When converting area, you must square the linear conversion factor.
\vspace{5pt}
\begin{center}
$1 \text{ mile} = 1,760 \text{ yards}$ \\
$1 \text{ sq mile} = (1,760)^2 \text{ sq yards} = 3,097,600 \text{ sq yards}$
\vspace{5pt}
$\text{Area in sq miles} = \dfrac{15,488,000}{3,097,600} = 5$
\end{center}
\vspace{5pt}
Note: Dividing by $1,760$ only once is a common mistake that leads to much larger, incorrect values.
\vspace{5pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{A}
\end{solution}

% Q2: Based on Electric Flux / Area Ratios (Very Hard)
\question
The electric flux through a surface is defined as the product of the electric field strength and the surface area perpendicular to the field. A composite surface consists of two adjacent squares: Square $A$ and Square $B$. The side length of Square $B$ is $4$ times the side length of Square $A$. An electric field with a strength of $35.00$ volts per meter passes uniformly and perpendicularly through the entire composite surface. If the total electric flux through the composite surface is $11,900$ volts$\cdot$meters, what is the electric flux, in volts$\cdot$meters, through Square $B$ only?
\begin{tasks}(2)
    \task 700
    \task 2,975
    \task 10,200
    \task 11,200
\end{tasks}

\begin{solution}
Let $s$ be the side length of Square $A$. Its area is $s^2$. \\
The side length of Square $B$ is $4s$, so its area is $(4s)^2 = 16s^2$.
\vspace{5pt}
\begin{center}
Total Area $= s^2 + 16s^2 = 17s^2$. \\
Total Flux $= \text{Field} \times \text{Total Area} = 35 \times 17s^2 = 595s^2$.
\vspace{5pt}
$11,900 = 595s^2 \implies s^2 = 20$.
\vspace{5pt}
Area of Square $B = 16s^2 = 16(20) = 320 \text{ m}^2$. \\
Flux through Square $B = 35 \times 320 = 11,200$.
\end{center}
\vspace{5pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{D}
\end{solution}

% Q3: Based on Sales/Income Tax Table (Very Hard)
\question
\begin{center}
\textbf{2025 State-Level Tax Distribution} \\
\vspace{5pt}
\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|}
\hline
 & \textbf{State Sales Tax} & \textbf{No State Sales Tax} \\ \hline
\textbf{State Income Tax} & 32 & 6 \\ \hline
\textbf{No State Income Tax} & 8 & 4 \\ \hline
\end{tabular}
\end{center}
The table above shows the distribution of 50 states based on their tax structures. To the nearest tenth of a percent, what percent of states that have \textbf{either} a state-level sales tax \textbf{or} a state-level income tax (but not both) do \textit{not} have a state-level income tax?
\begin{tasks}(2)
    \task 16.0\%
    \task 20.0\%
    \task 57.1\%
    \task 80.0\%
\end{tasks}

\begin{solution}
This is a conditional probability problem. First, identify the "restricted" population: states with \textit{either} sales \textit{or} income tax, but not both.
\vspace{5pt}
\begin{center}
(Income Tax ONLY) + (Sales Tax ONLY) \\
$6 (\text{No Sales}) + 8 (\text{No Income}) = 14 \text{ states}$.
\end{center}
\vspace{5pt}
Within this group of 14, we need the percentage that do \textit{not} have an income tax. These are the 8 states in the "No state income tax" row.
\vspace{5pt}
\begin{center}
$\text{Percentage} = \dfrac{8}{14} \approx 0.5714 = 57.1\%$.
\end{center}
\vspace{5pt}
Answer: \colorbox{yellow!100}{C}
\end{solution}
